Ayushman Card vs PMJAY: What is the difference?

Use this page when you feel confused between the card, the scheme name, and what each one actually means.

awareness and explainer

Direct answer

PMJAY is the health coverage scheme, while the Ayushman Card is the beneficiary card used to identify an eligible person under that scheme.

Quick distinction

PMJAY

PMJAY stands for Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana. It is the government health assurance scheme.

Ayushman Card

Ayushman Card is the card or beneficiary identity used when an eligible family member needs to verify their PMJAY record.

What people often mix up

Many users say “Ayushman Card” when they actually mean the whole PMJAY scheme. The card is one part of the larger scheme journey.

How to think about it

If your question is about meaning

Think about PMJAY as the scheme and Ayushman Card as the record or access layer attached to an eligible beneficiary.

If your question is about eligibility

The real question is whether your family appears in the official PMJAY data. The card itself does not create eligibility.

If your question is about download or use

You will usually move through an official beneficiary or app flow to verify the record and then access the card-related next step.
